Skill 1

📐 Story Structure — The Story Mountain!

Every great story follows a pattern called the Story Mountain! 🏔️

Think of it like climbing a mountain:

1️⃣ Orientation — Introduce WHO, WHERE, WHEN

2️⃣ Complication — Something goes WRONG! (The problem!)

3️⃣ Climax — The most exciting part! (The peak!)

4️⃣ Resolution — How the problem is solved

5️⃣ Ending — How the character feels or what they learned

Without a complication, there IS no story! The problem is what makes it exciting! 🔥

🏔️
The Story Mountain!
1️⃣ Orientation (who, where, when)
2️⃣ Complication (the problem!)
3️⃣ Climax (most exciting part!)
4️⃣ Resolution (problem solved)
5️⃣ Ending (lesson or feeling)
💡 Frankie's Tip!
In story writing challenges, you'll be asked to write a narrative. Having a clear structure with orientation → complication → resolution will score you great marks!

🔥 Sizzling Starts — Hook Your Reader!

The first sentence of your story is the MOST important! It needs to grab the reader's attention right away!

Frankie's favourite tricks for a sizzling start:

💬 Dialogue: "Run!" screamed Mia as the wave crashed behind them.

🎬 Action: The ground shook and the trees started to sway.

❓ Question: Have you ever wished you could fly?

😱 Sound effect: CRASH! The vase smashed into a thousand pieces.

Never start with "One day..." or "My name is..." — that's a Boring Beginning! 🥱

🔥
Sizzling Start Tricks!
💬 Start with DIALOGUE
🎬 Start with ACTION
❓ Start with a QUESTION
😱 Start with a SOUND
✏️ Compare These!
❌ One day I went to the beach and it was fun. ✅ "Watch out!" Dad shouted as the enormous wave raced towards us, spraying cold water everywhere. See the difference? The second one makes you want to read MORE! 🌊

Skill 2

🎭 Creating Amazing Characters!

Great stories have characters that feel REAL! How do you do that?

👀 Show, Don't Tell! This is Frankie's #1 rule!

Instead of TELLING the reader how a character feels, SHOW it through their actions, words, and body language!

❌ "Sam was scared." (Telling — boring!)

✅ "Sam's hands trembled as he peeked around the corner, his heart pounding like a drum." (Showing — exciting!)

Give your characters:

🏷️ A name and a detail (e.g., "curly-haired Maya")

💭 Thoughts and feelings

🗣️ Their own way of speaking

🎭
Show Don't Tell!
👀 SHOW through actions
💭 Give them THOUGHTS
🗣️ Let them SPEAK
👋 Use BODY LANGUAGE
💡 Frankie's Tip!
"Show Don't Tell" is the secret weapon of great writers! In writing challenges, it shows that you're a sophisticated writer. Try to use it at least 3 times in your story!

🎨 Painting Words — Descriptive Language!

Want to make your writing AMAZING? Use descriptive language — words that paint a picture in the reader's mind! 🎨

Adjectives — describing words (enormous, shimmering, ancient)

Similes — comparing using "like" or "as" ("fast AS a cheetah", "sparkled LIKE diamonds")

Alliteration — same starting sound ("slippery, slimy snake")

Onomatopoeia — sound words (CRASH! WHOOSH! SIZZLE!)

Use your 5 senses! Describe what characters see, hear, smell, taste, and feel!

🎨🖌️
Word Painting Tools!
📝 Adjectives (enormous, tiny)
🔗 Similes (as brave as a lion)
🔤 Alliteration (silly sausage)
💥 Onomatopoeia (BANG!)

✏️ Sentences That Sing!

Great writers use different types of sentences to keep readers interested!

Short sentences = drama and excitement!
"She froze."

Long sentences = description and flow
"The golden sunlight streamed through the window, warming her face as the birds sang outside."

Mix them up! If all your sentences are the same length, it sounds like a robot talking! 🤖

Also use compound sentences — join two ideas with words like: and, but, so, because, however

✏️📝
Sentence Variety!
⚡ Short = DRAMA (She ran.)
🌊 Long = DESCRIPTION
🔀 MIX them up!
🔗 Use joining words (but, so)
✏️ See the Difference!
❌ I went to the park. I played on the swings. I had lunch. I went home. (boring — all short!) ✅ I raced to the park and jumped on the swings, pumping my legs higher and higher until I could see over the fence. After eating my sandwiches under the old oak tree, I headed home with grass stains on my knees and a big grin on my face. Much better! Different sentence lengths = interesting writing! 🎵

Skill 3

💬 Writing Dialogue — Let Them Talk!

Dialogue means when your characters speak! Adding dialogue makes your story come alive! 💬

Here are the rules:

1️⃣ Put speech marks " " around what the character says

2️⃣ Start a new line for each new speaker

3️⃣ Use interesting speech verbs — not just "said"!

Instead of "said", try: whispered, shouted, exclaimed, muttered, cried, laughed, replied, gasped

4️⃣ Add actions BEFORE or AFTER the speech:

Maya bit her lip. "I don't think we should go in there," she whispered.

💬🗣️
Dialogue Rules!
"" Use speech marks
↩️ New line = new speaker
🔤 Use varied speech verbs
🎬 Add actions with speech

Skill 4

🌟 Brilliant Endings!

The ending is what readers remember MOST! So make it brilliant! 🌟

Frankie's favourite ending tricks:

💭 Reflection: The character thinks about what they learned
"As I walked home, I realised that the biggest adventures begin with the smallest steps."

🔄 Circle Back: Connect the ending to the beginning
(If your story started at the beach, end at the beach too!)

❓ Cliffhanger: Leave the reader wondering!
"But when I turned around... nothing was there."

Never end with "The End." or "Then I woke up." — those are boring! 🥱

🌟
Ending Tricks!
💭 Reflection (what was learned)
🔄 Circle back to the beginning
❓ Cliffhanger (leave them wondering)
🚫 Never "The End" or "Woke up"!
🧠 Quick Quiz Time!

Which ending technique is this? "I looked at the old tree house one last time and smiled. We'd built it together, and even though I was moving away, the memories would stay forever."
