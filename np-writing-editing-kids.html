Station 1

📝 Sentence Check

Every sentence needs to make sense on its own.

A strong sentence usually has a who and a what happened.

Editors check for:

🔹 complete thoughts

🔹 capital letters at the start

🔹 punctuation at the end

🔹 no repeated boring sentence openings

Edit Example

went to the park

Mia went to the park.

📝
Sentence Signals
👤 Who is it about?
⚡ What happened?
🔠 Start with a capital
🛑 End with punctuation
🧠 Quick Quiz!

Which option is a complete sentence?

Station 2

🔗 Combining Sentences

Short sentences are useful, but too many can sound choppy.

Editors combine ideas with words like and, but, because, when, and so.

Edit Example

The rain started. We ran inside.

When the rain started, we ran inside.

Good editing uses sentence variety. Mix short, medium, and longer sentences so the writing flows.

🔗
Linking Words
➕ and adds ideas
⚖️ but shows contrast
💡 because gives a reason
⏰ when shows time
Railway Reminder
A writer who starts every sentence the same way can sound bumpy. Varying sentence openers makes the writing glide more smoothly, like a train on clean tracks.

Station 3

📚 Paragraph Place

A new paragraph helps the reader notice a new idea.

Start a new paragraph when there is:

🔹 a new topic

🔹 a new speaker in dialogue

🔹 a new time or place

Edit Example

Tom packed his bag. He raced to the bus stop. At school he painted a dragon.

Tom packed his bag and raced to the bus stop.

At school, he painted a dragon.

📚
Paragraph Clues
🧠 One main idea
🗣️ New speaker, new line
📍 New place, new paragraph
🕒 New time, new paragraph
🧠 Quick Quiz!

When should you begin a new paragraph?

Station 4

🪢 Cohesion Crossing

Cohesion means the writing sticks together smoothly.

Editors help ideas connect by using:

🔹 linking words like first, next, meanwhile, finally

🔹 pronouns like he, she, they, it

🔹 repeated key words only when needed

Edit Example

Mina found a map. Mina followed the map. Mina opened a gate.

Mina found a map. Then she followed it and opened a gate.

🪢
Cohesion Tools
➡️ then, next, after that
👤 pronouns stop repetition
🔁 repeat only important words
🌊 smooth flow helps readers

Station 5

🔍 Proofreading Platform

After editing ideas, do a final proofread.

Proofread for:

🔹 capitals

🔹 full stops, commas, question marks

🔹 apostrophes

🔹 spelling mistakes

Edit Example

yesterday we bakeed muffins, they were delicious

Yesterday, we baked muffins. They were delicious.

Read slowly. Reading aloud helps you hear missing words or awkward sentences.

Review

🧰 Editor's Checklist

Run through this checklist before you finish:
✅ Are my sentences complete?
✅ Did I combine ideas smoothly?
✅ Did I start new paragraphs when needed?
✅ Do linking words and pronouns help the writing flow?
✅ Did I proofread capitals, punctuation, and spelling?
Edie's Big Tip
Edit in layers. First fix meaning. Then fix sentence flow. Last, proofread punctuation and spelling. Trying to fix everything at once makes it harder to spot errors.
