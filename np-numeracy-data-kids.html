📊 Picture Graphs & Bar Graphs!

Graphs are pictures that show us data! They help us compare things quickly! 📊

Picture Graph (Pictograph) uses little pictures to show amounts:

🍎🍎🍎 = 3 apples

🍌🍌🍌🍌🍌 = 5 bananas

Sometimes one picture = 2 or more! If 🍎 = 2 apples, then 🍎🍎🍎 = 6 apples!

Bar Graph uses bars (rectangles) to show amounts. Taller bar = bigger number!

Reading a bar graph:

1️⃣ Read the title — what is the graph about?

2️⃣ Read the labels — what do the axes show?

3️⃣ Read the scale — what does each line mean?

4️⃣ Compare the bars — which is tallest/shortest?

📈 Tables & Line Graphs!

A table organises data in rows and columns. It's like a neat grid! 📋

Tables have headings at the top of each column to tell you what the data means.

A line graph connects dots with a line to show how something CHANGES over time! 📈

Line graphs are great for showing:

🔹 Temperature over a week

🔹 How tall a plant grows each day

🔹 How many books you read each month

If the line goes UP ↗️ = the amount is increasing

If the line goes DOWN ↘️ = the amount is decreasing

If the line stays FLAT → = the amount stayed the same

📈
Reading Tracker (Books)
📅 Jan: 3 books
📅 Feb: 5 books ↗️
📅 Mar: 5 books →
📅 Apr: 8 books ↗️
💡 Dash's Tip!
In challenge questions, always read the TITLE and LABELS first! They tell you what the graph is about. Then look at the numbers carefully — sometimes the scale counts by 2s, 5s, or even 10s! 🔢

🎲 Chance & Probability!

Chance is about how LIKELY something is to happen! 🎲

We use special words to describe chance:

⬛ Impossible — it will NEVER happen (rolling a 7 on a normal dice)

🟥 Unlikely — it probably WON'T happen (snow in summer in Sydney)

🟨 Even chance — it MIGHT or MIGHT NOT happen (flipping heads on a coin)

🟩 Likely — it will PROBABLY happen (the sun rising tomorrow)

🟦 Certain — it will DEFINITELY happen (Monday comes after Sunday)

Think of it like a scale from impossible to certain!

A fair game gives everyone an equal chance. If one person has a better chance, it's unfair! 🎯

🎲
The Chance Scale
⬛ Impossible (0% chance)
🟥 Unlikely (low chance)
🟨 Even chance (50/50)
🟩 Likely (high chance)
🟦 Certain (100% chance)

🎯 Spinners, Dice & Coins!

Let's investigate probability with real experiments! 🔬

Coin flip: 🪙 Heads or tails? There are 2 options, so each has an even chance (1 out of 2).

Dice roll: 🎲 There are 6 faces, so the chance of rolling any number is 1 out of 6.

What's the chance of rolling an EVEN number (2, 4, or 6)? That's 3 out of 6 = even chance!

Spinners: If a spinner has 4 equal sections (red, blue, green, yellow), the chance of landing on each colour is 1 out of 4.

But if 3 sections are red and 1 is blue? Red is more likely (3 out of 4)! 🎯

The more sections of one colour, the more likely it is to land there!

🎯
Probability Maths
🪙 Coin: 1 out of 2 (50%)
🎲 Dice: 1 out of 6
🎨 4-colour spinner: 1 out of 4
💡 More sections = more likely!
🧠 Quick Quiz!

A bag has 3 red balls and 1 blue ball. Which colour are you MORE LIKELY to pull out?

⚖️ Mass — Grams & Kilograms!

Mass is how heavy something is! We measure it in grams (g) and kilograms (kg). ⚖️

Grams (g) = for LIGHT things:

🔹 A paperclip ≈ 1 g

🔹 An apple ≈ 150 g

🔹 A slice of bread ≈ 30 g

Kilograms (kg) = for HEAVY things:

🔹 A bag of sugar = 1 kg

🔹 A cat ≈ 4 kg

🔹 A child ≈ 25–35 kg

The magic number: 1 kg = 1000 g (just like 1 kilometre = 1000 metres!)

To convert: 2 kg = 2 × 1000 = 2000 g

And: 3500 g = 3500 ÷ 1000 = 3.5 kg

⚖️
Mass Units
🪶 Light things → grams (g)
🏋️ Heavy things → kilograms (kg)
🔄 1 kg = 1000 g
💡 "kilo" means 1000!
💡 Dash's Tip!
Remember: "kilo" ALWAYS means 1000! Kilogram = 1000 grams. Kilometre = 1000 metres. Kilolitre = 1000 litres. Once you know "kilo = 1000", you can convert anything! 💪

🥤 Volume & Capacity — Litres!

Capacity is how much liquid a container can hold! We measure it in millilitres (mL) and litres (L). 🥤

Millilitres (mL) = for SMALL amounts:

🔹 A teaspoon ≈ 5 mL

🔹 A juice box ≈ 250 mL

🔹 A can of soft drink = 375 mL

Litres (L) = for BIG amounts:

🔹 A bottle of milk = 1 L

🔹 A bucket ≈ 10 L

🔹 A bath ≈ 150 L

The magic number: 1 L = 1000 mL

To convert: 2 L = 2000 mL

And: 1500 mL = 1.5 L

🥤
Capacity Units
💧 Small amounts → mL
🪣 Big amounts → L
🔄 1 L = 1000 mL
🥛 1 milk bottle = 1 L
🧠 Quick Quiz!

🧭 Position & Direction!

A detective always knows WHERE things are! Let's learn about position and direction! 🧭

Position words:

🔹 Left, right, above, below

🔹 Between, next to, behind, in front of

🔹 Inside, outside, beside, opposite

Compass Directions:

⬆️ North (N) — up on a map

⬇️ South (S) — down on a map

➡️ East (E) — right on a map

⬅️ West (W) — left on a map

Memory trick: "Never Eat Soggy Weetbix!" — N, E, S, W going clockwise! 🥣

🧭
Compass Rose
⬆️ North = Up on map
➡️ East = Right on map
⬇️ South = Down on map
⬅️ West = Left on map
📖 Fun Fact!
In Australia, the sun rises in the EAST and sets in the WEST — just like everywhere else! But because we're in the Southern Hemisphere, the sun is towards the NORTH (not south like in Europe)! 🌏☀️

📍 Grid References & Turns!

Grid references tell us the exact position on a grid — like coordinates on a treasure map! 🗺️

We write them as (column, row) — always go ACROSS first, then UP!

Example: (3, 2) means go 3 across and 2 up!

Memory trick: "Along the corridor, then up the stairs!" 🏠

Now let's learn about Turns:

🔹 Quarter turn = 90° (like a corner)

🔹 Half turn = 180° (facing the opposite way)

🔹 Three-quarter turn = 270°

🔹 Full turn = 360° (back to where you started!)

Turns can go clockwise ↻ (same as clock hands) or anticlockwise ↺ (opposite)!

📍
Turns & Degrees
↱ Quarter turn = 90°
↶ Half turn = 180°
↺ Three-quarter = 270°
🔄 Full turn = 360°
🧠 Quick Quiz!

You're facing North. You make a quarter turn clockwise. Which direction are you now facing?
