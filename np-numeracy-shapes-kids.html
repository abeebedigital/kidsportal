Mission 1

🍕 Fractions — Sharing Fairly!

A fraction is a part of a whole! When you share a pizza equally, you're using fractions! 🍕

The TOP number is the numerator — how many parts you HAVE.

The BOTTOM number is the denominator — how many EQUAL parts in total.

Common fractions:

🔹 1/2 = one half (1 out of 2 equal parts)

🔹 1/4 = one quarter (1 out of 4 equal parts)

🔹 3/4 = three quarters (3 out of 4 parts)

🔹 1/3 = one third (1 out of 3 equal parts)

Equivalent fractions are fractions that look different but are the SAME amount:

🔹 1/2 = 2/4 = 4/8 (they're ALL one half!)

🍕
Fraction Pizza!
⬆️ Numerator = parts you have
⬇️ Denominator = total equal parts
🟰 1/2 = 2/4 = 4/8
🍕 Bigger denominator = smaller pieces

🔢 Decimals — The Dot That Matters!

Decimals are another way to write fractions! They use a decimal point (dot).

The number AFTER the dot shows parts of a whole:

🔹 0.1 = one tenth = 1/10

🔹 0.5 = five tenths = 5/10 = one half!

🔹 0.25 = twenty-five hundredths = 1/4

🔹 0.75 = seventy-five hundredths = 3/4

Think of money! 💰

🔹 $1.50 = one dollar and 50 cents (half a dollar!)

🔹 $0.25 = 25 cents (a quarter of a dollar!)

Decimals and fractions are the SAME thing, just written differently! 🤯

🔢💰
Decimal World!
🟰 0.5 = 1/2 = one half
🟰 0.25 = 1/4 = one quarter
🟰 0.1 = 1/10 = one tenth
💰 Money uses decimals!
💡 Shaper's Tip!
Challenge questions often ask you to put decimals in order. Remember: 0.5 is BIGGER than 0.25 (even though 5 looks smaller than 25!). Think of money — 50 cents is more than 25 cents! 💰

Mission 2

🔢 Patterns & Algebra — What Comes Next?

Patterns are sequences that follow a rule! 🧩

Number patterns:

🔹 2, 4, 6, 8, ? → Rule: +2 → Answer: 10

🔹 5, 10, 15, 20, ? → Rule: +5 → Answer: 25

🔹 100, 90, 80, 70, ? → Rule: −10 → Answer: 60

Number sentences (equations):

🔹 7 + __ = 12 → Think: 12 − 7 = 5

🔹 __ × 3 = 15 → Think: 15 ÷ 3 = 5

Balance / Equivalence:

🔹 7 + 3 = __ + 5 → Both sides must equal 10, so __ = 5

Finding the pattern rule is like being a detective! 🔍

🔍🧩
Pattern Detective!
📈 Growing: +2, +5, +10...
📉 Shrinking: −2, −5, −10...
⚖️ Balance: both sides equal
🔍 Find the rule!

Mission 3

📏 Length, Area & Perimeter!

Time to measure! Grab your ruler and let's explore! 📏

ConceptWhat It MeansHow to Find It
Length 📏How long something isMeasure with a ruler! Units: cm, m, km
Perimeter 🏃The distance AROUND a shapeAdd ALL the sides together
Area 🟦The space INSIDE a shapeCount the squares, or Length × Width

Perimeter example: A rectangle with sides 5cm and 3cm:

🔹 Perimeter = 5 + 3 + 5 + 3 = 16 cm

Area example: A rectangle 5cm long and 3cm wide:

🔹 Area = 5 × 3 = 15 cm² (square centimetres)

Remember: Perimeter = AROUND (fence) 🏃, Area = INSIDE (carpet) 🟦

📖 Fun Fact!
An easy way to remember the difference: Perimeter = think "perimeter fence" (goes AROUND). Area = think "area rug" (covers the INSIDE). Challenge questions love asking about both! 🎯

Mission 4

⏰ Time — Reading Clocks!

Time is everywhere — let's master it! ⏰

Analogue clocks have two hands:

🔹 Short hand = HOUR (moves slowly)

🔹 Long hand = MINUTES (moves faster)

Key times to know:

🔹 O'clock = long hand on 12

🔹 Half past = long hand on 6 (30 minutes)

🔹 Quarter past = long hand on 3 (15 minutes)

🔹 Quarter to = long hand on 9 (45 minutes)

Digital clocks show time with numbers:

🔹 3:00 = three o'clock

🔹 3:30 = half past three

🔹 3:15 = quarter past three

🔹 3:45 = quarter to four

⏰🕐
Clock Reading!
🕐 Short hand = hour
🕐 Long hand = minutes
🌅 AM = morning
🌙 PM = afternoon/night
🧠 Quick Quiz!

If the short hand is on 7 and the long hand is on 6, what time is it?

Mission 4

⏱️ Elapsed Time & Calendars!

Elapsed time means "How long did something take?" ⏱️

🔹 If a movie starts at 2:00 and ends at 4:30, it was 2 hours and 30 minutes long.

🔹 If school starts at 9:00 and lunch is at 12:30, that's 3 hours 30 minutes.

Trick: Count forward from the start time!

Calendars:

🔹 7 days in a week

🔹 52 weeks in a year

🔹 12 months in a year

🔹 365 days in a year (366 in a leap year!)

Days in each month:

🔹 30 days: April, June, September, November

🔹 31 days: Jan, Mar, May, Jul, Aug, Oct, Dec

🔹 28 or 29 days: February

📅⏱️
Time Facts!
⏱️ Elapsed = time passed
📆 7 days = 1 week
📅 12 months = 1 year
🗓️ 365 days = 1 year
💡 Shaper's Tip!
Challenge questions often test elapsed time! Use the "count forward" method: If it starts at 10:45 and takes 1 hour 30 minutes, count: 10:45 → 11:45 (+1hr) → 12:15 (+30min). Answer: 12:15! ⏰

Mission 5

🦋 Symmetry & Angles!

Symmetry means one half is a mirror image of the other! 🦋

A line of symmetry divides a shape into two matching halves.

🔹 A square has 4 lines of symmetry

🔹 A rectangle has 2 lines of symmetry

🔹 A circle has infinite lines of symmetry!

🔹 A butterfly is symmetrical! 🦋

Angles measure how much something turns:

🔹 Right angle = 90° (like a corner of a book 📖)

🔹 Acute angle = less than 90° (sharp and pointy!)

🔹 Obtuse angle = more than 90° (wide and open)

🔹 Straight angle = 180° (a flat line)

🦋📐
Symmetry & Angles!
🪞 Symmetry = mirror halves
📐 Right angle = 90°
📌 Acute = less than 90°
📏 Obtuse = more than 90°
📖 Fun Fact!
You can check for right angles using the corner of a piece of paper! If the corner fits perfectly, it's a right angle (90°). Challenge questions often ask you to identify angle types! 📐

Mission 6

🔲 Nets — Unfolding 3D Objects!

A net is what a 3D object looks like when you UNFOLD it flat! 🔲

Imagine cutting open a cardboard box and laying it flat — that's a net! 📦✂️

Common nets:

🔹 Cube net = 6 squares joined together (looks like a cross!)

🔹 Rectangular prism net = 6 rectangles

🔹 Cylinder net = 2 circles + 1 rectangle

🔹 Cone net = 1 circle + 1 fan shape

🔹 Pyramid net = 1 square + 4 triangles

Challenge tip: When you see a net, imagine FOLDING it up in your mind. Which faces would touch? Where would the edges meet? 🧠

📦✂️
Unfolded Shapes!
🧊 Cube = 6 squares
🥫 Cylinder = 2 circles + 1 rectangle
🍦 Cone = 1 circle + 1 fan
🔺 Pyramid = 1 square + 4 triangles
