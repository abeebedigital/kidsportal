# NP Competition Weekly Pattern

Use the same competition shell every week:

- `25` questions
- `60` minutes
- `1` winner
- winner rule: `highest correct answers`, then `fastest time`
- validity: exactly `7 days`

## Weekly structure

Keep the question count stable so results stay comparable:

- `7` Reading
- `6` Conventions of Language
- `7` Numeracy
- `5` Writing

## Question slot map

Use this slot plan when preparing each week:

1. Reading literal
2. Reading text feature
3. Reading inference
4. Reading heading/caption/diagram
5. Reading author purpose
6. Reading deeper inference
7. Reading text-type clue
8. Grammar parts of speech
9. Grammar tense consistency
10. Grammar or sentence structure
11. Punctuation capitals/full stops
12. Punctuation quotation marks
13. Spelling homophones
14. Spelling error identification
15. Spelling visual/phonetic pattern
16. Numeracy place value
17. Numeracy expanded form
18. Numeracy compare/order numbers
19. Numeracy addition
20. Numeracy subtraction
21. Numeracy multiplication or division
22. Numeracy fraction or decimal
23. Numeracy money/change
24. Writing narrative craft
25. Writing persuasive or editing craft

## 10-week rotation

### Week 1: Balanced Baseline
- Goal: broad mixed paper with even difficulty
- Best for: first live competition week

### Week 2: Reading and Language Lift
- Extra challenge in inference, purpose, grammar, and spelling
- Numeracy stays straightforward

### Week 3: Numeracy Power Week
- Harder place value, operations, fractions, and money items
- Reading and writing stay medium difficulty

### Week 4: Narrative and Persuasive Week
- Writing items become more demanding
- Reading questions include stronger inference clues

### Week 5: Conventions Accuracy Week
- Higher focus on editing, punctuation, homophones, and tense control
- Good week for proof-reading style questions

### Week 6: Real-World Numeracy Week
- Money, measurement, data, and multi-step operations
- Reading uses factual report passages

### Week 7: Analytical Reading Week
- More main-purpose and “between the lines” questions
- Writing focuses on clear sequencing

### Week 8: Speed and Fluency Week
- Slightly shorter passages
- Cleaner arithmetic and quick recognition items
- Useful for comparing time performance

### Week 9: Mixed Mastery Week
- Combine stronger distractors across all domains
- This should feel like the most “competition” style paper

### Week 10: Grand Final Week
- Best overall balance
- Include one standout question from each major skill area
- Use polished certificate language and stronger leaderboard messaging

## Difficulty ladder

Use this progression to keep the weekly competition fair:

- Weeks `1-2`: easier entry
- Weeks `3-4`: medium difficulty
- Weeks `5-7`: medium to challenging
- Weeks `8-10`: strongest mixed competition sets

## Writing question pattern

Rotate the five writing slots like this:

- Week `1`: character/setting, complication, persuasive word choice, sequencing, editing
- Week `2`: character detail, story tension, persuasive opening, conclusion choice, proofreading
- Week `3`: descriptive sentence, problem sentence, strong argument, paragraph order, editing
- Week `4`: narrative opening, complication clue, persuasive device, connective choice, revision choice

Repeat the pattern with fresh content after Week 4.

## Numeracy mini-rotation

Keep these inside the seven numeracy slots:

- `3` number/algebra questions
- `2` operations questions
- `1` fraction/decimal question
- `1` money or measurement/data question

## Naming pattern

Use a predictable file and key structure:

- file: `np-competition-week-02.html`
- key: `weekly-competition-2026-04-08`
- title: `The Weekly Skills Cup`
- label: `Week 2 Competition`

## Winner message pattern

Keep the leaderboard message consistent:

- `Winner: highest score first`
- `Tie-breaker 1: shortest completion time`
- `Tie-breaker 2: earliest submission time`
