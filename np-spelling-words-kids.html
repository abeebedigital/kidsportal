🔊 Phonics — Vowel Teams!

Vowel teams are when two vowels work together to make ONE sound! 🤝

Remember this rule: "When two vowels go walking, the first one does the talking!"

This means the first vowel says its NAME (long sound) and the second stays quiet!

Examples:

🔹 ai → rain, paint, train

🔹 ea → teach, read, clean

🔹 oa → boat, coat, road

🔹 ee → tree, see, free

But watch out — English is sneaky! "ea" can say /e/ too: bread, head! 😅

🔊
Vowel Teams!
🔹 ai = long A (rain)
🔹 ea = long E (teach)
🔹 oa = long O (boat)
🔹 ee = long E (tree)

🔊 Consonant Blends & Digraphs!

Blends = two or three consonants that you can hear BOTH sounds:

🔹 blue, crab, strong, flag, spoon

Digraphs = two letters that make ONE new sound:

🔹 sh → ship, fish

🔹 ch → chip, church

🔹 th → think, the

🔹 wh → whale, when

🔹 ph → phone (sounds like "f"!)

Tricky digraph: "gh" can say /f/ at the end → enough, cough, laugh

🤝
Blends vs Digraphs
🔊 Blends: hear BOTH sounds
🔗 Digraphs: ONE new sound
📞 ph = /f/ (phone)
😤 gh = /f/ (enough, laugh)
💡 Ollie's Tip!
If you see "ph" in a word, it almost ALWAYS sounds like "f"! Phone, photo, elephant, dolphin — they all have the "ph = f" pattern! 📸🐘🐬

🔧 Prefixes & Suffixes — Word Building!

You can build NEW words by adding parts to a base word! 🧱

Prefix = added to the FRONT (changes meaning)

🔹 unhappy = NOT happy

🔹 rewrite = write AGAIN

🔹 disagree = NOT agree

🔹 misspell = spell WRONGLY

Suffix = added to the END (changes word type)

🔹 hopeful = full of hope

🔹 careless = without care

🔹 teacher = one who teaches

🔹 slowly = in a slow way

🔧🧱
Word Building Blocks!
⬅️ Prefix = FRONT (un-, re-, dis-)
➡️ Suffix = END (-ful, -less, -er)
🎯 Base word = the middle part
🔢 prefix + base + suffix = new word!
📖 Fun Fact!
Challenge questions often test whether you know what prefixes and suffixes mean. If you see "un-" it almost always means "NOT"! Unhappy, unable, unfair, unusual — they ALL mean "not" something! 🧠

👯 Homophones — Sound-Alike Twins!

Homophones are words that sound the SAME but have different spellings and meanings! 👯

These are super tricky because you can't tell the difference when you HEAR them — only when you SEE them!

Here are the most important ones to know:

🔹 their (belongs to them) / there (a place) / they're (they are)

🔹 your (belongs to you) / you're (you are)

🔹 to (direction) / too (also/very) / two (the number 2)

🔹 hear (with ears) / here (this place)

🔹 where (a place?) / wear (clothes) / we're (we are)

👯
Sound-Alike Twins!
👂 Sound the SAME
📝 Spelled DIFFERENTLY
💡 Mean DIFFERENT things
⚠️ A super common challenge skill!

💡 Ollie's Top Trick!
If a word has an apostrophe (like they're, you're, we're), it ALWAYS means two words squeezed together! Try replacing it with the two words — if it makes sense, it's correct! "They're happy" → "They are happy" ✅

🤫 Silent Letters — Shhh!

Some letters in English are silent — they're there but you don't say them! 🤫

Here are the sneakiest silent letters:

🔹 Silent K: know, knife, knight, knee

🔹 Silent W: write, wrong, wrap, sword

🔹 Silent B: climb, thumb, lamb, comb

🔹 Silent G: gnat, gnome, sign

🔹 Silent H: hour, honest, ghost

The trick? When you're spelling, say the silent letter in your head! "KNIFE" → say "kuh-NIFE" quietly to remember the K! 🧠

🤫
Silent Letter Club!
🔇 K is silent before N (know)
🔇 W is silent before R (write)
🔇 B is silent after M (climb)
🔇 Say them in your HEAD!
