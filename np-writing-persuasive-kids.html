📣 What is Persuasive Writing?

Persuasive writing tries to convince the reader to agree with YOUR point of view! 📣

It's like being a lawyer in a courtroom — you present your case with strong arguments!

The Structure:

1️⃣ Introduction — State your opinion clearly!

2️⃣ Reason 1 — Your strongest argument + evidence

3️⃣ Reason 2 — Another good argument + evidence

4️⃣ Reason 3 — One more argument (bonus!)

5️⃣ Counter-argument — Address the OTHER side

6️⃣ Conclusion — Restate your opinion strongly!

Key rule: Don't just give your opinion — give REASONS and EVIDENCE!

⚖️
Persuasive Structure!
1️⃣ State your opinion
2️⃣ Give 2-3 reasons + evidence
3️⃣ Address the other side
4️⃣ Conclude strongly!

📣 Counter-Arguments — The Secret Weapon!

A counter-argument is when you mention what the OTHER side thinks — then KNOCK IT DOWN! 💥

This shows you've thought carefully about BOTH sides. It makes your argument even STRONGER!

How to write one:

Step 1: "Some people might say that..."

Step 2: "However, this is wrong because..."

Example:

Topic: School uniforms should be compulsory

"Some people argue that uniforms take away individuality. However, uniforms actually bring students together as a community. Students can express themselves through their personality, not their clothes."

Useful phrases: Some may argue... On the other hand... While some believe... Despite this... Nevertheless...

🛡️
Counter-Argument Shield!
1️⃣ State the OTHER side's view
2️⃣ Say "However" or "But"
3️⃣ Explain why YOUR side is better
🏆 Game changer in writing challenges!

📄 Paragraphing — Organise Your Ideas!

A paragraph is a group of sentences about ONE main idea! Think of paragraphs as rooms in a house — each room has a purpose! 🏠

The TEEL Structure:

🔵 Topic Sentence — introduces the main idea

🟢 Explain — explain what you mean

🟡 Evidence — give an example or proof

🔴 Linking Sentence — connect to next paragraph

When to start a new paragraph:

✅ New idea or topic

✅ New time or place

✅ New speaker (in dialogue)

✅ Your conclusion

📄
TEEL Paragraph!
🔵 T = Topic sentence
🟢 E = Explain
🟡 E = Evidence/Example
🔴 L = Linking sentence

📄 Cohesion — Weaving Sentences Together!

Cohesion means making your writing FLOW smoothly — like a river, not a bumpy road! 🌊

Use connectives (linking words) to connect your ideas:

TypeConnectivesExample
Addingalso, furthermore, in addition, moreoverPets teach responsibility. Furthermore, they provide companionship.
Contrastinghowever, on the other hand, despite, althoughHomework helps learning. However, too much can be stressful.
Cause/Effecttherefore, consequently, as a result, becauseStudents feel happier. Therefore, they learn better.
Time/Orderfirstly, secondly, finally, meanwhileFirstly, it saves money. Secondly, it helps the environment.
Concludingin conclusion, overall, to sum up, ultimatelyIn conclusion, schools should have more sport.
📝 Writing Level-Up!
Markers look for connectives! Using "firstly", "furthermore", and "in conclusion" shows you can organise ideas — and can boost your mark from average to great! 🌟

💎 Vocabulary — Power Words!

Great writers don't just say things — they say them with POWER WORDS! 💎

Compare these sentences:

❌ "It was a nice day." → BORING!

✅ "It was a magnificent day." → WOW!

❌ "The dog was big." → DULL!

✅ "The dog was enormous." → VIVID!

The Upgrade Rule: Replace boring words with exciting ones!

🔹 said → exclaimed, whispered, declared, announced

🔹 good → excellent, outstanding, brilliant, superb

🔹 bad → terrible, dreadful, appalling, awful

🔹 happy → delighted, ecstatic, thrilled, overjoyed

🔹 sad → devastated, heartbroken, miserable, gloomy

💎
Word Upgrade!
❌ nice → wonderful!
❌ big → enormous!
❌ said → exclaimed!
❌ went → dashed!

💎 Sensory & Figurative Language!

Sensory language uses the FIVE senses to paint pictures with words! 🎨

👀 Sight: The crimson sunset painted the sky

👂 Sound: The leaves rustled in the breeze

👃 Smell: The aroma of fresh bread filled the kitchen

👅 Taste: The lemon was tangy and refreshing

🖐️ Touch: The sand was warm beneath her toes

Figurative Language adds EXTRA power! ✨

🔵 Simile: fast like a cheetah / as brave as a lion

🟣 Metaphor: He IS a rock (he doesn't move)

🟡 Personification: The wind whispered through the trees

🟠 Alliteration: Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers

🎨
Language Paint Palette!
🔵 Simile: like / as
🟣 Metaphor: IS the thing
🟡 Personification: object acts human
🟠 Alliteration: same starting sounds

✏️ A Persuasive Essay — Example!

Topic: "Schools Should Have Longer Lunch Breaks"

🔵 Introduction: I strongly believe that schools should extend lunch breaks to at least one hour. There are several compelling reasons why this change would benefit every student.

🟢 Reason 1: Firstly, longer breaks give students more time for physical activity. Research shows that children who exercise during lunch perform better in afternoon classes. Furthermore, active children are healthier and happier.

🟡 Reason 2: Secondly, students need adequate time to eat a proper meal. Currently, many children rush through lunch and don't eat enough. Consequently, they feel tired and can't concentrate.

🔴 Counter-argument: Some may argue that longer lunches mean less class time. However, studies demonstrate that well-rested, well-fed students actually learn MORE in less time.

🟣 Conclusion: In conclusion, longer lunch breaks would improve students' health, happiness, and learning. It is time for schools to make this important change.

💡 Spot the Tools!
Can you find: connectives (firstly, furthermore, consequently, however, in conclusion), emotive words (compelling, happier), expert evidence (research shows, studies demonstrate), and a counter-argument? That's a top-scoring essay! 🏆

Bonus

✏️ Killer Openings & Closings!

Your opening and closing are the MOST important parts! The opening hooks the reader. The closing leaves them thinking! ✨

Strong Openings:

❓ Rhetorical question: "Have you ever wondered why...?"

😮 Shocking fact: "Every year, 8 million tonnes of plastic enter our oceans."

💬 Bold statement: "Without a doubt, homework should be banned."

🎭 Imagine: "Imagine a world where every child could..."

Strong Closings:

🔁 Circle back: Refer to your opening again

📣 Call to action: "It's time to make a change!"

❓ Rhetorical question: "Can we really afford to ignore this?"

🎯 Restate opinion: "It is clear that... and we must act now."

🎣
Hook Your Reader!
🎣 Opening = HOOK the reader
❓ Use a rhetorical question
📣 Closing = Call to action
🔁 Circle back to the start!
