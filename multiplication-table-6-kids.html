The Full Multiplication Table of 6

Look at the whole table first. This is our map.

Big idea: when we multiply by 6, we count in 6s.

So if we see 6 × 4, we think: “6, 12, 18, 24.” The answer is 24.

Read each row slowly. Point with your finger if that helps. We want your eyes, ears, and brain to work together.

Remember: times 6 means “count by 6s”.

Quick Review

Let’s lock the pattern into your memory.

Here is the whole trick in one sentence:

When you multiply by 6, count in 6s.

If the number is 8, then 6 × 8 = 48.

If the number is 12, then 6 × 12 = 72.
