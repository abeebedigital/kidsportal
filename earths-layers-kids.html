Chapter 1

What's Inside the Earth? 🤔

Imagine you could slice the Earth in half, just like cutting an apple! 🍎

You would see that the Earth has 4 main layers:

1. The Crust — the thin, hard outside (where we live!)

2. The Mantle — thick, hot, and gooey

3. The Outer Core — super hot liquid metal!

4. The Inner Core — the hottest, most amazing ball of solid metal at the very centre!

Each layer gets hotter and hotter as you go deeper. Let's explore them all!

p.3
Layer 1

🏔️ The Crust — Our Home!

"We start right here!" says Rocky. "The Crust is the layer you're standing on right now!"

The crust is the thinnest layer of the Earth. Under the oceans, it's only about 5 kilometres thick. Under the mountains, it can be up to 70 kilometres thick!

If the Earth were an apple, the crust would be just the thin skin on the outside! 🍎

The crust is made of rocks and soil. All our mountains, valleys, oceans, and everything we see is part of the crust!

And guess what? The crust isn't one piece — it's made of huge puzzle pieces called tectonic plates that fit together and slowly move around!

🏔️
The Crust
📏 5–70 km thick
🧩 Made of tectonic plates
🪨 Made of rocks and soil
🌡️ Cool on top, warm below
🧩 Fun Fact!
The tectonic plates move about 2 to 5 centimetres every year — that's about as fast as your fingernails grow! Slowly, slowly, they're always on the move! 💅🏔️
p.4
Layer 1

🏔️ Two Types of Crust!

Rocky says, "Did you know there are actually two types of crust?"

Oceanic crust is under the oceans. It's thinner (about 5 to 10 kilometres) but heavier, made of a dark rock called basalt.

Continental crust is under the land where we live. It's thicker (up to 70 kilometres!) but lighter, made of a rock called granite.

When tectonic plates bump into each other, amazing things happen — mountains push up, earthquakes shake, and sometimes volcanoes erupt! 🌋

That's the crust showing us it's alive and moving!

p.5
Layer 2

🔥 The Mantle — Thick and Hot!

"Deeper we go!" Rocky shouts excitedly. "Welcome to the Mantle!"

The mantle is the thickest layer of the Earth — it's almost 2,900 kilometres deep! That's like driving in a car for a whole day without stopping!

The mantle is made of very hot rock. It's so hot that the rock becomes soft and gooey, like very thick honey or warm toffee! 🍯

This gooey rock moves very, very slowly in big circles called convection currents. Hot rock rises up, cools down, then sinks again — round and round!

These movements are what push the tectonic plates around on top! 🔄

🔥
The Mantle
📏 2,900 km thick (the biggest layer!)
🌡️ 1,000°C to 3,700°C hot!
🍯 Rock moves like thick honey
🔄 Moves in slow circles
🌋 Fun Fact!
When hot rock from the mantle pushes up through cracks in the crust, it comes out as LAVA from a volcano! So volcanoes are like windows into the mantle! 🌋🔥
p.6
Layer 2

🔥 Mantle Secrets!

Rocky wipes his brow. "It's getting warm down here! Let me tell you more cool things about the mantle!"

The mantle has two parts:

The upper mantle is closer to the crust. The very top part is so stiff that it sticks to the crust — together they're called the lithosphere!

The lower mantle is deeper and even hotter. The rock here is squeezed so tightly by all the weight above it!

Even though the mantle rock moves, it moves really slowly — only a few centimetres each year. You wouldn't even notice!

The mantle makes up about 84 percent of the Earth's total volume! It's by far the biggest layer! 🏆

Gooey, hot rock!
🔝 Upper mantle: stiffer
🔽 Lower mantle: softer, hotter

💎 Contains diamonds deep down!

Layer 3

🌊 The Outer Core — Liquid Metal!

"Hold on tight!" says Rocky. "We're entering the Outer Core — and it's made of liquid metal!"

The outer core is about 2,200 kilometres thick. It's made of super-hot melted iron and nickel — just like the metals in coins!

The temperature here is between 4,400 and 5,000 degrees! That's hotter than any oven you can imagine! 🌡️

But here's the most amazing part — this swirling liquid metal creates Earth's magnetic field! 🧲

The magnetic field is like an invisible shield around our planet. It protects us from harmful rays from the Sun! Without it, life on Earth would be impossible!

🌊
The Outer Core
📏 2,200 km thick
🌡️ 4,400–5,000°C!
🪙 Made of iron & nickel
🧲 Creates magnetic field!
🧭 Fun Fact!
A compass needle points north because of the magnetic field created by the outer core! So every time you use a compass, you're using the power of liquid metal deep inside the Earth! 🧭🌊
p.8
Layer 3

🌊 The Magnetic Shield!

Rocky explains, "The spinning liquid iron in the outer core works like a giant magnet!"

This creates an invisible force field around the Earth called the magnetosphere. It stretches far out into space!

The magnetosphere stops dangerous particles from the Sun from reaching us. It's like Earth's very own superhero cape! 🦸

Sometimes these particles hit the magnetosphere and create beautiful coloured lights in the sky called the Northern Lights (Aurora Borealis)! 🌌

So the outer core doesn't just sit there — it's actually protecting all living things on our planet every single day!

🧲🌍🛡️
Earth's invisible shield!
🛡️ Magnetosphere protects us
🌌 Creates Northern Lights!
🧭 Makes compasses work
🔄 Liquid metal spins and swirls

p.9
Layer 4

💎 The Inner Core — Earth's Heart!

"We've made it to the very centre!" Rocky cheers. "Welcome to the Inner Core!"

The inner core is a solid ball of mostly iron and nickel, about 1,200 kilometres across. It's about the same size as the Moon! 🌙

Even though it's incredibly hot — about 5,400 degrees (as hot as the surface of the Sun!) — it stays SOLID because the pressure from everything above it is so enormous!

Imagine the weight of all the rock, metal, and everything on top squeezing it together. That pressure keeps it solid even though it's super hot!

The inner core spins a tiny bit faster than the rest of the Earth. Scientists think it's like a planet within a planet!

💎
The Inner Core
📏 1,200 km across
🌡️ 5,400°C — hot as the Sun!
💎 Solid iron & nickel
🌙 About the size of the Moon!
💎 Fun Fact!
Scientists believe there might be a giant crystal of iron at the very centre of the inner core! Some think it might even be a single enormous crystal — the biggest crystal in the world! 💎✨
p.10
Layer 4

💎 Inner Core Mysteries!

Rocky whispers, "The inner core is full of mysteries that scientists are still trying to solve!"

No one has ever been to the inner core — it's 6,371 kilometres below your feet right now! The deepest hole anyone has ever dug is only about 12 kilometres. We have a looong way to go!

So how do we know about it? Scientists use earthquake waves! When an earthquake happens, the waves travel through the Earth. By studying how these waves travel, scientists figured out what's inside! 🔬

It's like tapping on a watermelon to check if it's ripe — you listen to the sound to learn what is inside! 🍉

The inner core was only discovered in 1936 by a clever scientist named Inge Lehmann from Denmark!

🔬🌍🔊
Listening to earthquake waves!
📏 6,371 km to the centre
🕳️ Deepest hole: only 12 km
🔊 Earthquake waves tell the story
👩‍🔬 Discovered by Inge Lehmann, 1936

🥚 Earth Is Like an Egg!

Rocky has a fun way to remember the layers! "Think of a boiled egg!" he says.

The eggshell is like the Crust — thin and hard on the outside!

The egg white is like the Mantle — thick and in the middle!

The egg yolk is like the Core — round and in the very centre!

Next time you eat a boiled egg, you can think about the Earth's layers! 🥚🌍
