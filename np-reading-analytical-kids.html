Skill 1

🔗 Cause & Effect — Why Did It Happen?

Every event has a CAUSE (why it happened) and an EFFECT (what happened as a result)! 🔗

Cause = the REASON something happens

Effect = the RESULT — what happens next

Example: "Sam didn't study for the test, so he got a low score."

🔹 Cause: Sam didn't study

🔹 Effect: He got a low score

Look for these signal words:

➡️ because, since, so, therefore, as a result, due to, consequently, this led to

Sometimes the effect comes FIRST! "The road was wet because it rained." (Effect first, then cause!)

🔗
Cause → Effect Chain!
❓ Cause = WHY?
💥 Effect = WHAT happened?
🔑 Signal: because, so, therefore
⚠️ Order can be swapped!
🧠 Quick Quiz!

"The flowers grew tall because they got plenty of sunlight." What is the CAUSE?

🔗 Chain Reactions!

Sometimes one cause leads to an effect, which becomes the cause for something ELSE! It's like dominoes! 🎲

Chain Example:

1️⃣ The alarm didn't ring (cause)

→ 2️⃣ Mia woke up late (effect → becomes new cause)

→ 3️⃣ She missed the school bus (effect → becomes new cause)

→ 4️⃣ She was late for school (final effect)

Challenge tip: Sometimes the question asks "What CAUSED this?" and the answer might be earlier in the chain — not the event right before it!

Always ask: "What started this whole thing?" 🔍

🎲
Domino Effect!
1️⃣ Alarm didn't ring
2️⃣ → Woke up late
3️⃣ → Missed the bus
4️⃣ → Late for school!
💡 Lexie's Detective Trick!
When you see a chain of events, draw arrows between them! This helps you SEE the connections. In challenge questions, many wrong answers are effects — but the question asked for the CAUSE! Read carefully! 🔍

⚖️ Fact vs Opinion — True or Just a Thought?

A FACT is something that can be PROVEN true. An OPINION is what someone THINKS or FEELS! ⚖️

TypeWhat It IsExamples
FACT ✅Can be checked and proven trueAustralia has 6 states. Water boils at 100°C.
OPINION 💭Someone's personal thought, belief, or feelingChocolate is the best flavour. Dogs are better than cats.

Signal words for opinions:

💭 I think, I believe, in my view, the best, the worst, should, beautiful, favourite, boring, amazing, everybody knows

🧠 Quick Quiz!

Which of these is an OPINION?

Skill 2

⚖️ Tricky Fact vs Opinion!

Some statements are SNEAKY — they LOOK like facts but are really opinions! 😈

Tricky Example 1:

"Everyone knows that pizza is the tastiest food."

→ OPINION! "Everyone knows" doesn't make it a fact. "Tastiest" is a personal judgement! 💭

Tricky Example 2:

"Scientists say you should drink 8 glasses of water a day."

→ This looks like a fact because it mentions scientists. But "should" makes it more of a recommendation (opinion).

The Test: Ask yourself — "Could someone disagree with this?" If YES, it's probably an opinion! 🤔

Also ask: "Can I check this in a book or a reliable source?" If YES, it's probably a fact! ✅

🕵️
The Disagree Test!
✅ Fact = can be PROVEN
💭 Opinion = could be DISAGREED with
⚠️ "Best/worst" = always opinion!
🔍 "Should/must" = often opinion!

📚 Vocabulary in Context — Word Detective!

Sometimes you see a word you DON'T know — but you can figure out its meaning from the context (the words around it)! 🔍

Strategy 1: Look at the sentence

"The ferocious lion roared and chased the zebra."

→ Clues: roared, chased → ferocious probably means fierce or scary!

Strategy 2: Look for synonyms nearby

"She was jubilant — so happy she could burst!"

→ "so happy" tells us jubilant means very happy!

Strategy 3: Look for opposites

"Unlike the timid rabbit, the bear was bold and brave."

→ Opposite of bold/brave → timid means shy or scared!

🔎
Context Clue Strategies!
1️⃣ Read the WHOLE sentence
2️⃣ Look for synonyms (similar words)
3️⃣ Look for antonyms (opposites)
4️⃣ Replace word — does it make sense?
🧠 Quick Quiz!

"The enormous elephant towered over the tiny mouse." What does "enormous" most likely mean?

📚 Multiple-Meaning Words!

Some words have MORE than one meaning — the context tells you which one! These are called homonyms! 🎭

WordMeaning 1Meaning 2
bat🏏 Something you hit a ball with🦇 A flying animal
bank🏦 Where you keep money🌊 The side of a river
light💡 What helps you see🪶 Not heavy
bark🐕 Sound a dog makes🌳 Outside of a tree trunk
ring💍 Jewellery for your finger📞 Sound a phone makes
right➡️ The opposite of left✅ Correct
📖 Reading Tip!
Challenge questions often ask "In this text, what does the word ___ mean?" Don't just pick ANY meaning — pick the one that fits the sentence! Try replacing the word with each answer choice and see which one makes sense! 🧪

📝 Text Types — Narrative!

Different texts are written for different purposes! There are 3 main types: 📝

Type 1: NARRATIVE 📖

Purpose: To entertain — tell a story!

Features:

🔹 Has characters, a setting, and a plot

🔹 Beginning → problem → resolution → ending

🔹 Uses past tense ("She walked...")

🔹 Has dialogue with speech marks

🔹 Uses descriptive language & emotions

Examples: fairy tales, adventure stories, picture books, myths, fables

How to spot it: Does it tell a STORY with characters? Is there a problem to solve? → It's a narrative! 📖

📖
Narrative = Story!
🎯 Purpose: ENTERTAIN
👤 Has characters
🗺️ Has setting & plot
💬 Has dialogue

📝 Informative & Persuasive Texts!

Two more important text types you'll see in challenge reading! 📝

Informative (Report) 📋Persuasive (Argument) 💬
PurposeTo INFORM — teach factsTo PERSUADE — convince you
FeaturesFacts, headings, diagrams, no opinionsOpinions, reasons, emotive language
LanguagePresent tense, factual, technical words"I believe", "should", "must", "the best"
StructureIntroduction → facts by topic → summaryOpinion → reasons → conclusion
ExamplesEncyclopaedia, news report, textbookLetter to the editor, advertisement, speech
🧠 Quick Quiz!

"All schools should ban junk food because it is unhealthy for children." What text type is this?

📝 Why Was This Text Written?

Challenge questions love asking: "What is the main purpose of this text?" 🎯

Here's Lexie's 3-question checklist:

1️⃣ Does it tell a story with characters? → To entertain (narrative)

2️⃣ Does it teach you facts about something? → To inform (informative)

3️⃣ Does it try to change your mind? → To persuade (persuasive)

Bonus types you might see:

📜 Procedural — gives instructions/steps (recipe, how-to guide)

📝 Recount — retells events that really happened (diary, news)

🔍 Explanation — explains HOW or WHY something works

🎯
Purpose Checklist!
📖 Story? → To entertain
📋 Facts? → To inform
💬 Change your mind? → To persuade
📜 Steps? → To instruct
💡 Lexie's Power Tip!
Some texts have MORE than one purpose! A recipe book might inform (teach facts about food) AND instruct (give steps). A persuasive letter might also include facts to support the argument. Look for the MAIN purpose — the BIGGEST reason the author wrote it! 🏆
