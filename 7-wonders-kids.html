Let's fly around the world and visit them all! ✈️🌍

Wonder 1

🏯 The Great Wall of China

Our first stop is China! 🇨🇳

Imagine a wall so long that if you walked along it every day, it would take you more than a year to reach the end!

The Great Wall is over 21,000 kilometres long — that's like going halfway around the whole Earth! 🌏

It was built by millions of workers over many, many years to protect China from invaders. They used stones, bricks, and even sticky rice as glue!

If you stood on the wall, you could see green mountains stretching as far as your eyes can see! 🏔️

🏯
Great Wall of China
📏 21,196 km long!
📅 Over 2,300 years old
🧱 Made of stone & brick
🍚 Sticky rice was used as glue!
🌟 Fun Fact!
The Great Wall is NOT visible from space with the naked eye — that's actually a myth! But it IS so amazing that astronauts always look for it! 👀🚀

Wonder 2

🏛️ Petra — The Rose City

Next, we fly to Jordan! 🇯🇴

Hidden between tall, red cliffs in the desert is a SECRET city called Petra!

People carved beautiful buildings right into the pink and red rock. That's why it's called the "Rose City"! 🌹

Petra is over 2,000 years old. Clever people called the Nabataeans built it. They even made a system to collect rainwater in the desert!

To enter Petra, you walk through a narrow, winding path between towering cliffs. It feels like entering a magical world! ✨

🏛️
Petra, Jordan
🌹 Called "Rose City"
⛏️ Carved into rock cliffs
📅 Over 2,000 years old
💧 Had clever water systems

Wonder 3

🗿 Christ the Redeemer

Now we fly to sunny Brazil! 🇧🇷

High up on a mountain called Corcovado, there stands an enormous statue with its arms stretched wide open!

It is called Christ the Redeemer, and it is 30 metres tall — that's as tall as a 10-story building! 🏢

The statue looks over the beautiful city of Rio de Janeiro. From up there, you can see beaches, forests, and the sparkling ocean!

It took 9 years to build, and it's made of concrete and a special stone called soapstone.

🗿
Christ the Redeemer, Brazil
📏 30 metres tall
⛰️ On top of Corcovado mountain
📅 Built in 1931
⚡ Gets struck by lightning!
⚡ Fun Fact!
Christ the Redeemer gets struck by lightning about 3 to 6 times every year! Don't worry — it has special lightning rods to stay safe! ⚡🗿

Wonder 4

⛰️ Machu Picchu — City in the Clouds

Our next stop is Peru! 🇵🇪

Way up high in the Andes mountains, hidden by clouds and mist, is an ancient city called Machu Picchu!

It was built by the amazing Inca people over 500 years ago. They built it so high up that clouds float right through the buildings! ☁️

The most incredible thing? The stones fit together perfectly — without any glue or cement! It's like the world's most amazing jigsaw puzzle! 🧩

Machu Picchu was hidden from the world for hundreds of years until an explorer found it in 1911!

⛰️
Machu Picchu, Peru
🏔️ 2,430 metres up in the mountains
📅 Built around 1450
🧩 Stones fit without glue!
🔍 Rediscovered in 1911

Wonder 5

🏛️ Chichen Itza — The Pyramid

Let's fly to Mexico! 🇲🇽

In the jungles of Mexico stands a magnificent pyramid called El Castillo — that means "The Castle" in Spanish! 🏰

It was built by the clever Maya people over 1,000 years ago. The pyramid has exactly 365 steps — one for each day of the year! 📅

And here's the magical part: Every spring and autumn, the sunlight creates a shadow that looks like a giant snake sliding down the pyramid! 🐍

If you clap your hands near the pyramid, the echo sounds like a bird singing! How cool is that? 👏🐦

🏛️
Chichen Itza, Mexico
📐 365 steps (1 for each day!)
🐍 Shadow snake trick!
👏 Echo sounds like a bird
📅 Over 1,000 years old
🐍 Fun Fact!
The Maya people were so good at maths and science that they created their own calendar! The pyramid of Chichen Itza is like a giant calendar made of stone! 📅🏛️

Wonder 6

🏟️ The Colosseum — Battle Arena!

Next stop — Italy! 🇮🇹

In the city of Rome stands the biggest amphitheatre ever built — the Colosseum!

Imagine a stadium that could hold 50,000 people — that's a LOT of cheering fans! 📣

Almost 2,000 years ago, people came here to watch gladiators compete. The Colosseum even had a special floor that could be flooded with water for pretend sea battles! 🌊⚔️

Today, part of the Colosseum has crumbled, but it still looks magnificent and millions of people visit every year!

🏟️
Colosseum, Rome, Italy
👥 Held 50,000 people!
📅 Almost 2,000 years old
🌊 Could be flooded for sea battles
🏗️ Took only 8 years to build
🧠 Quick Quiz Time!

Wonder 7

🕌 The Taj Mahal — A Love Story

Our last wonder is in India! 🇮🇳

The Taj Mahal is the most beautiful building many people have ever seen! It's made of sparkling white marble and shines like a jewel! 💎

A king called Shah Jahan built it because he loved his wife Mumtaz Mahal SO much. When she passed away, he wanted to build the most beautiful place in the world for her. 💕

It took 22 years and over 20,000 workers to build! The walls are decorated with precious gems and beautiful flower patterns.

The coolest thing? The Taj Mahal changes colour during the day — it looks pink at sunrise, white during the day, and golden at sunset! 🌅

🕌
Taj Mahal, India
💎 Made of white marble
💕 Built for love
📅 Took 22 years to build
🌅 Changes colour with sunlight!
💎 Fun Fact!
The Taj Mahal has 28 different types of precious and semi-precious gemstones set into the marble! Gems came from places as far as Tibet, Egypt, and Sri Lanka! 💎🌏

Wonder 7

🕌 More About the Taj Mahal!

The Taj Mahal sits on the banks of the Yamuna River in a city called Agra.

Around the Taj Mahal there are four tall towers called minarets. They lean slightly outward — on purpose! If they ever fell, they would fall AWAY from the Taj Mahal to protect it! 🏗️

The gardens in front have beautiful fountains and the Taj Mahal reflects perfectly in the water. It looks like there are TWO Taj Mahals! 🪞

About 8 million visitors come to see it every year. That's like the whole population of a big city!

🕌🪞🕌
Reflection in the water!
🗼 4 minarets lean outward
🌳 Beautiful gardens & fountains
👥 8 million visitors per year
🏗️ 20,000+ workers built it
