Skill 1

📌 Finding the Main Idea

Every story or paragraph has a main idea — it's the most important thing the writer wants you to know!

Think of it like this: if a story were a pizza 🍕, the main idea is the base. Everything else — the toppings — are just details that support it!

Here's how to find the main idea:

1️⃣ Ask yourself: "What is this mostly about?"

2️⃣ Look at the first sentence — it often tells you!

3️⃣ Check if the other sentences support that idea.

🍕
Main Idea = Pizza Base!
🍕 Main idea = the BASE
🧀 Details = the TOPPINGS
❓ Ask: "What is this mostly about?"
👀 Check the first sentence!
💡 Lexie's Tip!
If you can explain what a paragraph is about in ONE sentence, you've found the main idea! Try it: "This paragraph is mainly about ___."

Skill 1

📌 Main Idea vs Details

Now let's learn the difference between the main idea and supporting details!

Main idea = the BIG point (what it's mostly about)

Details = the small facts that help explain it

Look at this example:

🍎 Main idea: "Eating fruit is good for you."

📝 Detail 1: "Apples have lots of vitamins."

📝 Detail 2: "Bananas give you energy."

📝 Detail 3: "Oranges help you fight colds."

See? The details all support the main idea! 🎯

🎯
Main Idea in the Centre!
🎯 Main idea = the bullseye
📝 Details = the rings around it
🤔 Ask: "Does this fact support the big idea?"
⭐ Details give proof & examples
📖 Fun Fact!
In reading challenges, many questions ask you to find the main idea of a paragraph. Now you know exactly how to do it! You're already ahead! 🌟

Skill 2

👤 Characters, Settings & Events

Every story has three important parts. Lexie calls them the Big Three! 🌟

👤 Characters = the people or animals IN the story (WHO)

🏡 Setting = WHERE and WHEN the story happens

📅 Events = WHAT HAPPENS in the story (the actions!)

When you read, always ask yourself these three questions:

1️⃣ WHO is in this story?

2️⃣ WHERE and WHEN does it happen?

3️⃣ WHAT happens? (First, next, last?)

👤🏡📅
The Big Three!
👤 Characters = WHO
🏡 Setting = WHERE & WHEN
📅 Events = WHAT HAPPENS
🔢 Events have an ORDER!
💡 Lexie's Tip!
To remember events in order, use the words: FIRST, NEXT, THEN, FINALLY. These are called sequence words and they'll help you retell any story!

Skill 2

🔢 Sequencing — What Happened When?

Great readers can retell a story in the right order. Let's practise with Maya's story about Biscuit! 🐱

Here's what happened, step by step:

FIRST: Maya found a kitten under a bush in the park.

NEXT: She picked it up and wrapped it in her scarf.

THEN: She carried it home and gave it warm milk.

FINALLY: The kitten purred happily and Maya named it Biscuit.

See how each event follows the one before it? That's called sequencing! 📖

1️⃣➡️2️⃣➡️3️⃣➡️4️⃣
Events in Order!
1️⃣ FIRST — what happened first?
2️⃣ NEXT — what came after?
3️⃣ THEN — what happened then?
4️⃣ FINALLY — how did it end?
📖 Fun Fact!
In reading challenges, you might be asked "What happened AFTER...?" or "What happened BEFORE...?" — knowing the sequence helps you answer these perfectly! 🎯

Skill 3

🔍 Making Inferences — Reading Detective!

Now for Lexie's FAVOURITE skill — making inferences! 🕵️

Sometimes, the author doesn't tell you EVERYTHING directly. You have to figure things out from clues in the text!

This is called reading between the lines. 🔍

Here's the magic formula:

📖 What the text says + 🧠 What you already know = 💡 An inference!

For example: "Sam grabbed his umbrella before leaving the house."

The text doesn't SAY it's raining... but you can infer it is! ☔

🕵️
Reading Detective Formula!
📖 Text clues (what it says)
➕ PLUS
🧠 Your brain (what you know)
💡 = An INFERENCE!
💡 Lexie's Tip!
When a question says "How can you tell...?" or "What does this suggest...?" — they're asking you to make an inference! Use clues + your brain! 🧠✨

Skill 3

🔮 Predicting — What Happens Next?

Another awesome inference skill is predicting! 🔮

When you predict, you use clues from the story to guess what might happen next.

Good predictions are based on evidence — not just random guessing!

Let's try! Read this:

"Dark clouds rolled across the sky. The wind started howling and the trees bent sideways. Mum called out from the door, 'Come inside quickly!'"

What do you think will happen next? 🤔

You can predict that a storm is about to hit! The clues are: dark clouds, howling wind, and Mum calling to come inside.

🔮
Prediction Power!
👀 Look for CLUES in the text
🧠 Think about what you know
🔮 Make a smart prediction
📖 Read on to check if you're right!
🧠 Quick Quiz Time!

"Tom put on his boots, grabbed his fishing rod, and packed sandwiches." What is Tom probably about to do?
